What is the Tour de France?

Introduction

The Tour de France is an annual men's multiple-stage bicycle race primarily held in France, while also occasionally passing through nearby countries. Celebrated as one of cycling's Grand Tours, it is typically held in July and covers approximately 3,500 kilometers (2,200 mi).

History

The Tour de France was first organized in 1903 to increase sales for the newspaper L'Auto. It has been held annually since, with the exception of times during the two World Wars.

Format

The race is divided into 21 stages, each with varying difficulties, including flat stages, mountain stages, and time trials. Riders compete both for individual stage wins and for the overall fastest time across all stages.

Teams and Jerseys

  • Yellow Jersey: Worn by the overall race leader.
  • Green Jersey: Awarded for sprint points.
  • Polka-Dot Jersey: Given to the best climber.
  • White Jersey: For the best young rider.

Famous Riders

Some of the most famous riders include Eddy Merckx, Bernard Hinault, and more recently, Chris Froome. These athletes are celebrated for their multiple tour wins and their impact on the sport.
